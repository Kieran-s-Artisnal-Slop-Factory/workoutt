# Records, weight tracking, and achievements

Workoutt keeps track of your progress in three ways: your **body weight**
over time, your **personal records** (PRs) in each exercise, and
**achievements** you unlock along the way. This guide explains all three.

## Body-weight tracking

If you enter a current weight during setup (or tick "Track body weight" in
Settings → Preferences), the **Records** page gets a Body weight section:

- **Log your weight** with the input at the top — once a day is plenty.
  Logging twice on the same day just updates that day's entry.
- **The graph** shows your recent trend. By default it shows the last
  3 months; you can change that window (1 month up to all time) in
  Settings → Preferences → "Body-weight chart range". A shorter window
  makes recent changes easier to see.
- **The history list** below the graph always keeps every entry you've
  ever logged, no matter the graph's range — and you can edit or delete
  any entry there if you made a typo.

Weigh-ins also feed the per-program summaries: the app can show you how
your weight changed over each run of a program.

## Personal records (PRs)

A **personal record** is your best-ever result in an exercise. You never
enter PRs by hand — the app works them out automatically from the workouts
you complete. What counts as a "best" depends on how the exercise is
measured:

| Exercise type | Records tracked |
| --- | --- |
| Weight + reps | Heaviest weight lifted; biggest single set (weight × reps) |
| Reps only | Most reps in one set |
| Timed (e.g. plank) | Longest time |
| Distance (e.g. running) | Longest distance; best pace |

Each record also keeps its **history** — every time you beat it — so you
can see your progression, not just today's number.

On the **Records** page:

- **Search** finds any exercise quickly.
- **The star (☆)** on a record card pins it to a *Highlighted PRs* section
  at the top — handy for the lifts you care most about.
- **New PRs are celebrated** right after you finish a workout, in the
  workout summary.

### All Time vs. By Program

The dropdown at the top of the records list has two views:

- **All Time** — your best ever, across everything you've logged.
- **By Program** — your best *during one specific program*. Pick a program
  from the list and the records recalculate for just that period. This is
  useful because your all-time best might be from years ago; By Program
  shows what you actually achieved in this training block. It also shows
  your body-weight change for each time you ran that program.

## Achievements

Achievements are little milestones that unlock automatically as you train.
Find them on the **Achievements** page; new ones also pop up in the
summary right after a workout.

There are three kinds:

1. **Account achievements** — earned once, ever. Examples: completing your
   first workout, reaching 10 / 30 / 100 total workouts, training on 7
   different days within one week, or writing your first end-of-workout
   note.
2. **Exercise achievements** — earned separately for each exercise, and
   matched to how it's measured. A few examples:
   - *One tonne* — lift a total of 1,000 kg across all your sets of one
     exercise (it adds up faster than you'd think!). Higher tiers at 10 and
     50 tonnes.
   - *Rep machine* — 1,000 total reps of a bodyweight exercise.
   - *Long hauler* — cover a marathon's distance (42.2 km) in total on a
     running exercise.
3. **Program achievements** — tied to your programs:
   - Some are earned **per run** — e.g. lifting a tonne of total volume
     within one run of a program, or finishing a run with every scheduled
     workout completed. Run the program again and you can earn them again.
   - Others are earned **once per program** no matter how many times you
     run it — e.g. completing the same program 2, 3, or 5 times.

On the Achievements page, locked achievements show a **progress bar** so
you can see how close you are; earned ones show the date you got them.
Roman numerals (I, II, III) mark the tiers of multi-level achievements.

Two nice-to-know details:

- Achievements are based on your full history, so if you've been training
  for a while before this feature existed, your earned milestones appear
  the first time you open the Achievements page.
- Achievements are never taken away — once earned, it's yours, even if you
  later edit or delete old workouts.
