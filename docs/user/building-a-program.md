# Building a program

This guide walks you through creating your first training program in
Workoutt — including what all the pieces mean if you're new to fitness.

## First, the concepts

Workoutt organizes your training in three layers. Understanding them makes
everything else click:

### Exercises

An **exercise** is a single movement — push-ups, bench press, running,
planks. Each exercise records what you actually measure when you do it:

- **Weight + reps** — you lift a weight a number of times. One "rep"
  (repetition) is doing the movement once; lifting a bar 8 times is 8 reps.
- **Reps only** — bodyweight movements like push-ups, where there's no
  extra weight to record.
- **Time** — things you hold or sustain, like a plank.
- **Distance / distance + time** — running, cycling, rowing.
- **Weight + time** — for example, a weighted carry or weighted plank.

### Workout templates

A **workout template** is a reusable session plan: *which* exercises you'll
do and *how many sets* of each. A "set" is one group of effort — for
example, "3 sets of 10 push-ups" means: do 10 push-ups, rest, do 10 more,
rest, do 10 more.

You might see **SS** next to some exercises — that means they're
**supersetted**: you do them back-to-back with no rest in between, then
rest after the pair. It saves time and keeps intensity up.

Templates are plans, not diaries. When you actually work out, the app
copies the template and you log what you *really* did — which might differ
from the plan, and that's fine.

### Programs

A **program** is a schedule that spreads your workout templates across the
week for a number of weeks — e.g. "3 times a week for 8 weeks, on Monday,
Wednesday, and Friday, rotating through Push day, Pull day, and Leg day."
Once started, the app puts every session on your calendar. If you miss a
day, the schedule automatically shifts that workout forward — you don't
have to do anything.

## The easy way: the guided walkthrough

If you're starting fresh, let the app build a program for you:

1. When you first set up the app, say **yes** to "Want help setting up
   your first program?" (You can also reach it any time from the Programs
   page.)
2. **Tell it your experience level.** Be honest — "Beginner" gives you 3
   days a week with simpler sessions; more experience adds days and
   intensity. You can change everything later.
3. **Pick a plan style:**
   - *Cardio focused* — mostly running/cycling/rowing, one strength day.
   - *Strength (Calisthenics)* — bodyweight only, no equipment needed.
   - *Strength (Dumbbells + Calisthenics)* — a pair of dumbbells is enough.
   - *Strength (Full gym)* — assumes access to barbells and machines.
   - *Build my own from scratch* — blank forms, for when you know what you
     want.
4. **Review the exercises** it picked (step 1). Remove any you can't do or
   don't like; add your own. Tick **"Add Unused Exercises to Database"** if
   you'd like the full exercise library available — it also lets you pick
   from those extra exercises in the next step.
5. **Review the workouts** (step 2). Each card is one session; you can
   rename it, change set counts, add or remove exercises.
6. **Confirm the schedule** (step 3): how many days a week, which days,
   and for how many weeks. Tick "Start this program today" and hit
   **Create** — your first workout appears on the home page.

Nothing is saved until that final Create, so feel free to go back and
forth.

## Building everything by hand

Prefer full control? Create the pieces in the same order:

1. **Exercises** page → add each movement you'll use (name, the body parts
   it works, and how it's measured). If the page is empty, the
   **"Load default exercises"** button fills it with a ready-made library
   you can build from.
2. **Workouts** page → create a template: name it (e.g. "Upper body A"),
   add exercises in the order you'll do them, and set how many sets each
   gets. You can also set targets (like "3 sets of 8–10 reps") and group
   exercises into supersets here.
3. **Programs** page → create a program: pick which workout templates it
   rotates through (the order matters — that's the order they'll appear on
   your calendar), how many sessions per week, your preferred days, and
   the length in weeks. Then start it.

## Living with a program

- **Your next workout** always shows on the home page — hit *Start* when
  you're at the gym (or living room). *Read more* shows what's planned and
  what you did last time.
- **The calendar** on the home page shows the whole month — completed
  workouts get a ✓. You can drag a workout to a different day to
  reschedule it.
- **Missed a day?** The app bumps the workout forward to your next
  preferred day automatically. If you want to skip it entirely, use *Skip*
  instead — skipped workouts don't reschedule.
- **Editing a running program** (Programs page) changes the future only:
  your completed history is never rewritten.
- When the program's weeks are up, you can start it again — records and
  achievements track each run separately (see
  [records-and-achievement.md](./records-and-achievement.md)).

## A note for beginners

You don't need the "perfect" program — you need one you'll actually
follow. Three days a week of basic movements, done consistently for eight
weeks, beats any elaborate plan that fizzles in two. Start with the guided
walkthrough on Beginner, and adjust as you learn what you enjoy.
