# Reminders

Workoutt can send you two optional reminders:

- **Workout day** — a nudge on a day you have a workout scheduled.
- **Did you forget to finish?** — if you start a workout and leave it open for
  45 minutes without finishing, a gentle check so your sets don't go unsaved.

They're **off by default** and never required — Workoutt works fine without
them.

## Turning them on

1. Go to **Settings → Reminders** and tick **Enable reminders**.
2. Your browser will ask permission to show notifications — choose **Allow**.
3. Pick what you want:
   - **Next workout reminder**, and the **time** you'd like it (e.g. 8:00 am).
   - **"Did you forget to finish?"** after 45 minutes.

Reminders are delivered by your **sync server**, so they work even when the app
is closed — as long as your device has been set up for notifications (below).

## "This device" — enabling on each browser

Notifications are granted per browser and per device, so each phone, tablet, or
computer you want reminders on needs to be enabled once. The Reminders card
shows the status for the device you're on:

- **✓ Push is on for this device** — you're all set. You can **Disable here** to
  stop this one device without affecting the others.
- **Enable on this device** — tap it to turn on notifications for this browser.
- If it says notifications are **blocked**, your browser has denied them for the
  site — re-enable notifications for Workoutt in your browser's site settings,
  then reload.

For the best results, **install Workoutt to your home screen / desktop** first.
On iPhone and iPad this is required before notifications can be turned on.

## Good to know

- **Timing is approximate.** Reminders may arrive a minute or two after the
  target time.
- **The reminder time uses your server's clock.** For a home server that's your
  own timezone; if your server lives elsewhere, adjust the time accordingly.
- **While the app is open**, the "did you forget to finish?" reminder always
  works, even without server notifications set up.
- **Nothing is shared.** Reminders are generated from your own workout data on
  your own server — there's no third-party account or tracking.
- **Turning reminders off** (the master switch, or **Disable here** for one
  device) stops them immediately; your workouts and settings are untouched.
